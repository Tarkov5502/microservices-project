The Gardener

~ a parable about letting go of the hoe ~

You've inherited an old estate. The previous head gardener has passed. You are the head gardener now — but the rules of the estate are strict: the head gardener does not plant. The head gardener does not weed. The head gardener writes the plan.

Apprentices carry out your written rules. Plants grow. Plants die. Storms strike. Weeds spawn. The apprentices continuously check: does the garden look like the plan? If not, they act.

You will be tempted to do things directly. Resist. Once you trust the system — really trust it — the garden will tend itself, and you'll see why it works the way it works.
