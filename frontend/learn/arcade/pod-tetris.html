WHAT IS KUBERNETES?

60 seconds of context, then the game.

Kubernetes is the system that runs modern apps on many computers reliably. Companies like Netflix, Spotify, your bank, and your government all use it. It does many things — networking, storage, secrets, deployments, monitoring. Today you'll play just one of them: the scheduler.

📦

Pods are workloads

An app gets packaged into a "pod." Each pod asks for some CPU and some memory. In real K8s, a pod runs containers — for our purposes, it's just a thing that needs space.

🖥

Nodes are machines

A "node" is a physical (or virtual) computer. It has finite CPU and memory. You arrange pods onto nodes — that's scheduling.

node-1
node-2
⚖

Constraints make it interesting

Replicas of the same service shouldn't share a node (anti-affinity). GPU workloads need GPU nodes (taints). Databases can't move (stateful). The game teaches each constraint by making you handle it.

🤔

You're the scheduler

In real K8s, an algorithm does this 1000s of times per second. Here, you do it. The mental model you build is the same one the algorithm uses.

What you won't see in this game: the rest of Kubernetes (networking, storage, deploys, the control plane). After Wave 10 we'll show you what was outside the scope.

THE REST OF KUBERNETES

You played the scheduler. Here's what else lives inside.

The game taught you bin packing, anti-affinity, taints, stateful workloads, priority + preemption, pod affinity, and the cluster autoscaler. Those are real concepts you'll see in production. Here's the bigger map — the organs you didn't touch.

⚙
Scheduler
Which node does each pod go on? You just played 30 minutes of it.
🔄
Control Loop
Kubernetes constantly compares "desired state" to "observed state" and reconciles. The game was turn-based; reality is continuous.
🌐
Services + Ingress
How traffic finds your pods, even as they're killed and replaced. Stable DNS names in front of changing pod IPs.
📋
Deployments + ReplicaSets
You don't usually create pods directly. You declare "I want 3 of these," and the controller maintains it.
📦
Containers + Images
A pod runs one or more containers, each from a Docker image. The game treated pods as primitive; they actually have layers underneath.
💾
Persistent Volumes
Where stateful pods actually keep their data. You felt the "can't move" symptom; PVCs are the underlying mechanic.
🩺
Health Probes
Liveness + readiness checks — Kubernetes asks pods "are you alive? are you ready?" continuously. → Play Pod Defender for this
🚀
Rolling Updates
Swap v1 pods for v2 without dropping traffic. → Try the practice mode
🔐
RBAC + Secrets
Who can do what to which resources. Plus secret/credential management via ConfigMaps and Secrets objects.

A working mental model of all of these is what makes someone a Kubernetes engineer. The scheduler is one organ. You've now touched it. The rest is one project, one outage, one weekend at a time.
